diversity

diversify postgres

The mission of the Diversity Committee of the United States PostgreSQL Association is to promote an inclusive environment in the PostgreSQL community through outreach programs and the support of educational endeavors for underrepresented PostgreSQL users in the United States.

participation

Current members of the United States PostgreSQL Association are encouraged to participate in the further development and growth of the Association through committee participation. Interested members should send their intent to diversity@postgresql.us for consideration.

diversity scholarship

The United States PostgreSQL Association manages a $10,000 fund for our Diversity Scholarship. The goal of the scholarship is to encourage diversity of participation in PostgreSQL conferences and events. 6 applicants have been granted scholarships to attend events such as PgDay Chicago and PgConf NYC in 2023. You can apply for the Diversity Scholarship using this application form.

You can apply to have some or all of your conference attendance costs (registration, hotel, airfare) covered. Just like submitting a talk, details count! Your application should include specifics of how attending the event will benefit you, and how you plan to share the benefit with others in the community.

The decision making process will be based on a mixture of criteria such as scholarship applicability, benefit to the appliant, their plan to give back to the community, seriousness of the application, logistical factors and budget remaining. Each committee member will vote on these applications and based on that we will reach out to you with an email confirming whether your application was accepted or rejected and the scholarship amount approved.

The Diversity Committee would like to thank our friends and members of the Django community for allowing us to review and borrow from their program.
